Some tips to help reduce anxiety from the HSE:

  • Eating and exercise. When people feel anxious they often forget about themselves. Eating healthy food and getting regular exercise will improve your health and wellbeing.
  • Relaxation. There are lots of ways to relax but some of us need to learn how. Different things work for different people, so you need to find what works for you. Yoga, pilates and meditation are very popular. There are classes available in most towns. They’re not for everyone, and not the only way to relax. Going for a walk, run with a friend or taking some time out for yourself are all methods of relaxing.
  • Self-talk. When you’re feeling anxious, remind yourself this is a feeling that will pass. Try to distract yourself by thinking about something different. See if you can concentrate on your breathing, focusing your attention elsewhere.
  • Talking about your anxiety. Bottling things up can increase how anxious you feel. It can be hard but if possible talk to a friend, family member or a GP about what’s making you feel anxious. The simple act of talking to someone outside the situation can help you to get some context on how you are feeling.
  • Give it time. Changes in behaviour don’t happen overnight. But you can learn to manage feelings of anxiety and not let them take over. Following the tips here will help and don’t forget to reach out to someone to talk about it.
  • Extra support for anxiety. If you’re feeling so anxious that it’s impacting on your day-to-day life, you might need some extra support. See face-to-face help to see who you can talk to if you decide you want outside help. Cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT) has been found to be effective in helping people manage anxiety. CBT helps you manage problems by thinking more positively. It frees you from unhelpful patterns of behaviour. People like your local GP or a clinical or counselling psychologist can help you. If they can't, then they may refer you to a specialist.

How to get to Salthill?

The walk to Sathill is approximately 27 minutes from the university which could be a good option if the day is nice and you want to clear your head before the exam. The drive to Salthill is only apporximatley 7 minutes without traffic. You can use the map provided in on the map page of this website or Google maps to help find your way. The Students Union does provide a bus service that is 2 euro each way from opposite the main gates of the university. The timetable is included below.

SU Bus Schedule

Parking in Salthill

Parking should not be an issue if you do decide to drive to an exam in Salthill. The Galway Bay Hotel has an underground car park that can be accessed via the back of the building and is free to use for students during their exams. Leisureland also has free on site parking.

What to do if you are sick the day of an exam?

If you don't feel well the day of the exam and feel like you will be unable to complete it, you must go to the college doctor the day of the exam in order to be allowed to defer that exam until the August exam period without capping. The university no longer forces you to defer all remaining exams if you have to defer one so please do contact the college health unit if you are unwell. Call (091) 492 604. More information regarding deferrals is available here

What to do if you cannot find your student card?

There is a 25 euro fine for each exam that you fail to present your student card in. To get another student card printed you must go to Student Information Desk (SID) and there is 20 euro reprint fee. SID opens early during the exam period to ensure students can have a valid student card before the first exam. More information about SID

How to do an MCQ

In order for the MCQ correcting machine to be able to read the MCQ students must use a black pen. If you have time, fill in the MCQ first with pencil and then go over the answers again with black pen. If you are unsure of an answer be sure to check if your exam has negaitve marking before hazarding a guess. It may be best to leave answers blank if you have no idea which answer(s) are correct.
